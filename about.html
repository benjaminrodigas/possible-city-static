Board of advisors

DIALLO POWELL
CIO OF FREELANCERS UNION, BUSINESS/STRATEGIC ADVISOR

Diallo has spent his career combining business strategy and emerging technologies to strengthen enterprises. He is accomplished in business development, strategic planning & execution, technology management, product development, and business process and methodology design.

Diallo Powell is the Chief Information Officer of Freelancers Union and its subsidiaries, which includes Freelancers Insurance Company (FIC) and Independent Workers Services. Freelancers Union is a national 170,000 member non-profit organization.

At Freelancers Union Diallo works closely with the CEO to support strategic planning and business development activities. He also oversees all technology and project management office (PMO) operations. In February of 2012, Diallo was appointed by the CEO to lead the development of the proposal and business plan that resulted in a $341MM funding award to launch CO-OPs in three states.

Heather McGrory
Senior Sustainability Strategist, Purpose Strategic Advisor

Heather has worked with the public and private sectors in early-stage strategic development to create successful projects and partnerships in the environmental, social justice and clean tech areas. At Purpose, a company that creates 21rst Century movements, Heather works with clients to implement new projects, create organizational shifts and develop strategic alliances and joint partnerships. The Ford Foundation, The Gates Foundation, The Bertha Foundation are some of Heather's past and current clients. Prior to Purpose, Heather worked at Natsource Asset Management where she managed several successful projects with the World Bank and governments of China, India and South Korea, at NASA doing climate impact studies for the UN and the City of New York and at the Albright Stonebridge Group working with corporations to create effective sustainability programs. Heather has a Masters in International Affairs from SIPA and a BA from Barnard College.

DEENAH LOEB
EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR CITY PARKS ASSOCIATION, VACANCY SPECIALIST

Deenah Loeb Executive Director, City Parks Association Philadelphia Deenah Loeb has over 25 years of program innovation and implementation experience in arts and the environment. With the City Parks Association, Ms. Loeb has developed numerous educational symposia and initiated LANDvisions, an idea-generating program to address vacancy in Philadelphia which included the Urban Voids design competition which CPA sponsored along with the Van Alen Institute Ms. Loeb holds a MLA from the University of Pennsylvania and a BA from Wesleyan University.

KATE MEEHAN
EDUCATION PARTNERSHIP/COMMUNICATIONS ADVISOR

Kate is a writer, editor, English professor and academic program administrator at St. Joseph’s College where she teaches contemporary literature and composition. Kate is the Associate Director of the ACES writing program which empowers immigrant and international scholarship students as they transition from math and science scholars and English language learners to confident and fluent academic writers and thinkers. Her writing pedagogy reflects an interest in directing students towards civic and community engagement through the writing and research process. Kate has volunteered as a writing instructor at Philly Spells Writing Lab, a non profit organization that provides free creative and expository writing instruction for school age children in inner Philadelphia. She graduated with a BA in Pre-18th CenturyLiterature, Classics and Inter-Arts (Studio Art/Art History), she earned her MA in Literature and Writing at the Bread Loaf School of English at Middlebury College.

Nathaniel Popkin
Journalist and Cultural Advisor

Nathaniel Popkin is a writer of the American urban experience, a narrator of the messy and violent ambition of the city street. Trained as a city planner at Penn’s Graduate School of Design (’94), Popkin’s writing often takes the reader onto the street, exploring the space where the built environment meets the metaphysical city of culture, ideas, and religion. In 2011-12, he was the guest architecture critic of the Philadelphia Inquirer.

Popkin is currently the senior writer for the documentary film series “Philadelphia: The Great Experiment” and the co-editor of Hidden City Daily, a web magazine that covers architecture, planning, public space, history, urban exploring, historic preservation, landscape architecture, urban design, and politics. He is the co-founder of Hidden City Philadelphia, an organization that pulls back the curtain on the city’s most remarkable places and connects them to new people, functions and resources.

BILL SAMUELS
ATTORNEY AT LAW W.R. SAMUELS LAW, PLLC, LEGAL COUNSEL

Bill Samuels advises clients on protecting, enforcing, and leveraging their intellectual property. Even more, Bill advises clients, largely start-up organizations, on realizing their goals from the point of conception of an idea, to committing that idea to practice and eventual commercialization or implementation. From business entity formation to internal corporate arrangements, IP commercialization to business considerations, Bill works with clients to find legal solutions for achieving their objectives efficiently and effectively.

Bill has worked with individuals, non-profits, and businesses in industries as varied as real estate, consumer packaged goods, clothing, retail, media, computer technology, and biotech. In each case Bill and his team tailor legal strategies and plans to achieve each respective client's goals.

JEYUUN KIM
CO-FOUNDER CRITTERCISM, START-UP ADVISOR/GURU

Jeeyun attended MIT where she graduated with a BS in Architectural Design and then received her M.Arch at UPenn. Her interest in designing experiences led her down the path of UI/UX and into startup-land. Crittercism's Mobile APM platform is used by thousands of companies, from indie developers to Fortune 100 retailers and businesses leveraging mobile devices for employee-facing access to lineof-business data. Her experiences as both a designer and start-up co-founder have been invaluable to Possible City.

ADDITIONAL COLLABORATORS

SARAH KATHLEEN PECK
COMMUNICATIONS EXPERT

Sarah is a storyteller, writer, and designer with an architecture background and a penchant for explaining how things work.She is the founder of the award-winning Landscape Urbanism website and leads the communications team for SWA Group, an international landscape architecture and urban design firm.

EMMALEIGH DOLEY
FOUNDER, WEST ROCKLAND STREET PROJECT / AUDIENCE ENGAGEMENT EDITOR, NEXT CITY

At Next City, Emmaleigh works on ways to engage readers with the Informal City Dialogues, Next City content, and other initiatives. With a background in public relations and communications, Emaleigh previously worked with dozens of Philadelphia-based nonprofits, design, museum, and culturalorganizations to elevate their profile through the media. She is co-organizer of a citizen-driven effort to revitalize one Philadelphia city block (W Rockland Street) through small-scale urban interventions; her civic work has been profiled in The Philadelphia Inquirer, on CNN, and in many other news outlets. She graduated from George Washington University (B.A., English). Emmaleigh connected with Douglas and Possible City through Twitter.

THADDEUS SQUIRE
FOUNDER OF HIDDEN CITY PHILADELPHIA AND CULTUREWORKS.

Thaddeus has worked across a wide variety of disciplines, from history and heritage to the fine and performing arts. His particular interest is in building creative collaborations and new business models for the cultural andcreative industries. Specifically he runs CultureWorks, an organization that provides shared resources and management for creative professionals. Douglas and Thaddeus met through Nathaniel Popkin, and he has expressed an interest in incubating Possible City as part of CultureWorks.

JESSE WILBUR

ANDI HANSEN
LECTURER IN LANDSCAPE ARCHITECTURE AT THE HARVARD GRADUATE SCHOOL OF DESIGN

In addition to her teaching and work on Visualizing Systems, Andrea’s current research and writing focus on landscape infrastructure strategies for deindustrialized cities and the development of mapping and visualization tools that link GIS, social media, and other web-based maps applications. Andi’s graduate work centered around her thesis based on vacancy.
